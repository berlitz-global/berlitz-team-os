# EF English Live - Pricing Analysis

## Pricing Model

Premium subscription for consumers with minimum 6-month commitment [MyEngineeringBuddy](https://www.myengineeringbuddy.com/blog/ef-education-first-reviews-alternatives-pricing-offerings-in-2026/). Custom enterprise pricing for corporate clients [EF Corporate Learning](https://corporatelearning.ef.com/en/). No free tier [EF English Live](https://englishlive.ef.com/en/). EF does not publicly display prices on their website; pricing requires account creation or a sales call [BestLanguageLearningApps](https://bestlanguagelearningapps.com/blog/ef-english-live-price-why-can-t-you-see-the-prices).

## Enterprise/B2B Pricing

- **Corporate plans:** Custom pricing, requires quote [EF Corporate Learning](https://corporatelearning.ef.com/en/)
- **Estimated per-seat cost:** $30-80/user/month depending on scope and volume (Confidence: Low, industry estimates)
- **Minimum seats:** Typically 50+ for corporate programs (Confidence: Low, not confirmed by EF)
- **Contract terms:** Annual or multi-year [EF Corporate Learning](https://corporatelearning.ef.com/en/)
- **Features included:** Admin dashboard, LMS integration, real-time progress reports, industry-specific content (250 topics, 18 industries), dedicated CSM, SLAs [EF Corporate Learning Solutions](https://corporatelearning.ef.com/en/solutions/)

## Government/Institutional Pricing

- **Government contracts:** Available in some markets; requires direct engagement [EF Corporate Learning](https://corporatelearning.ef.com/en/)
- **Educational institutions:** Partnerships available, including alumni pricing [EF English Live Alumni](https://englishlive.ef.com/en/plans-and-prices-alumni/)

## What the End User Actually Pays

- **A casual learner** pays ~$89-129/mo with a minimum 6- or 12-month commitment for access with group classes and private lessons - significantly more expensive than app-based competitors [MyEngineeringBuddy](https://www.myengineeringbuddy.com/blog/ef-education-first-reviews-alternatives-pricing-offerings-in-2026/).
- **A serious learner** pays ~$139/mo (12-month plan) for premium access with 8 private 1:1 lessons per month [MyEngineeringBuddy](https://www.myengineeringbuddy.com/blog/ef-education-first-reviews-alternatives-pricing-offerings-in-2026/).
- **An enterprise buyer** pays ~$30-80/user/month (estimated) for corporate training with full enterprise features, admin tools, and dedicated support (Confidence: Low).

**Key Insight:** EF English Live is 1.4-7x more expensive than Berlitz at every tier ($89-139/mo vs $19-99/mo) while offering only English and requiring long-term contracts with steep cancellation fees [MyEngineeringBuddy](https://www.myengineeringbuddy.com/blog/ef-education-first-reviews-alternatives-pricing-offerings-in-2026/) [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/englishlive.ef.com). Berlitz can position as the modern, more affordable alternative with AI avatar practice, credit-based flexibility, monthly commitments, and multi-language/multi-skill programs. For enterprise buyers, the pitch focuses on comparable enterprise features at potentially lower cost with significantly better AI technology.

## Methodology

Research conducted on 2026-06-05 using 8 web searches. Consumer pricing data is sourced from third-party review sites since EF does not publicly display prices. Enterprise pricing is estimated based on industry norms and is tagged Low confidence. Cancellation fee data comes from consumer complaint patterns on Trustpilot and independent review blogs. All claims are tagged with confidence levels in the Confidence Summary table above. EF's pricing opacity means all consumer price figures should be treated as approximate and region-dependent.
