**Quadrant Analysis:**

| Quadrant | Players | Berlitz Opportunity |
|----------|---------|---------------------|
| High AI + High Human (top-right) | **Berlitz (target)** | This is the unoccupied position. No competitor combines deep AI with deep human coaching today. |
| Low AI + High Human (top-left) | EF, Lingoda, Preply, Italki, Rosetta Stone, Babbel | Legacy human-teaching models vulnerable to AI disruption. Displacement targets. |
| High AI + Low Human (bottom-right) | Speak, ELSA, Praktika, TalkPal | Strong AI but no human coaching. Cannot serve enterprise or outcome-driven learners. |
| Low AI + Low Human (bottom-left) | Duolingo, Busuu | Gamification/content-first. Duolingo owns this quadrant at scale. |

**Key Insight:** Berlitz's target position (top-right) is genuinely unoccupied. The strategic imperative is to get there before Preply (moving right with AI) or Speak (moving up with B2B/teachers) can converge.

## Competitive Threats by Persona

### Hobbyist Hana (Plus, $19/mo)

**Primary threat:** Duolingo (free tier)
- Hana's casual commitment makes free alternatives extremely attractive
- Duolingo's gamification drives daily habit formation better than any competitor
- Risk: Hana never converts from Duolingo free to Berlitz Plus

**Secondary threats:** TalkPal ($4.99/mo), Praktika (~$8/mo)
- Cheaper AI conversation practice may be "good enough" for hobby learners

**Berlitz counter:** Free tier with unlimited AI practice must be compelling enough to hook Hana. The value-up from free to Plus ($19/mo) is the AI tutor sessions with structured feedback -- something Duolingo and TalkPal don't offer. Key metric: free-to-paid conversion rate.

### Professional Pedro (Pro, $49/mo)

**Primary threat:** Speak ($99/yr / ~$8/mo)
- Speak offers high-quality AI conversation practice at a fraction of Berlitz Pro pricing
- Pedro may decide AI-only practice is sufficient for work conversations

**Secondary threats:** Preply ($60-200/mo for tutoring), Babbel Live (~$57-107/mo)
- Human tutoring alternatives exist but at higher cost and inconsistent quality

**Berlitz counter:** Pedro needs measurable progress and efficiency -- not just practice. Berlitz Pro delivers 6 credits/mo for AI tutor + human sessions, structured CEFR progression, and learning analytics. The pitch: "You get what Speak offers (AI practice) plus what Preply offers (human tutoring) in one platform, for less."

### Career Carla (Career, $39/mo)

**Primary threat:** Preply Business (if employer-funded)
- If Carla's employer uses Preply Business, she may get human tutoring through work
- Preply's 5,000+ B2B clients make this a real pipeline threat

**Secondary threats:** EF English Live (if employer-funded), Babbel for Business
- Enterprise competitors can capture Carla through her employer's L&D budget

**Berlitz counter:** Carla needs business English specifically -- role-play for negotiations, presentations, interviews. Berlitz's business content and skills programs are unique differentiators. Plus, the Career tier includes a free skill module that no competitor bundles. The pitch targets both Carla and her employer: "Business communication outcomes, not just language lessons."

### Executive Elena (Premium, $99/mo)

**Primary threat:** EF English Live (~$130-150/mo)
- EF offers a comparable premium experience with live teachers and self-study
- EF's global presence and corporate brand may appeal to executives

**Secondary threats:** Lingoda (~$89-139/mo), Preply premium tutors ($200-400/mo)
- Elena can get premium human teaching from multiple sources

**Berlitz counter:** Elena demands quality and efficiency. Berlitz Premium delivers 16 credits/mo (8 AI tutor + 4 human sessions), which is more human access than EF at a lower price ($99 vs $130-150), plus unlimited AI practice between sessions. The 140-year Berlitz brand and 97.8% instructor satisfaction close the trust gap.

### Enterprise Buyer Ben (Enterprise, custom)

**Primary threat:** Rosetta Stone (enterprise/government incumbent)
- Rosetta Stone has deep enterprise features (FedRAMP, SCORM, SCIM) and government contracts
- Switching costs are high for enterprises already on Rosetta Stone

**Secondary threats:** Preply Business ($1.2B valuation, 5K clients), EF English Live (global corporate)
- Both have established enterprise sales motions and client bases

**Berlitz counter:** Ben needs ROI -- time to fluency, pre/post assessment, HRIS integration. Rosetta Stone's technology is outdated; EF is expensive; Preply lacks curriculum quality. Berlitz offers modern AI + certified human coaching with ROI dashboards and credit-based budget control. The pitch: "Prove learning outcomes to your CFO with data, not just seat usage."

## Win/Loss Positioning Guide

### vs Duolingo
**When we lose:** Casual learners who want free and fun. Don't compete here.
**When we win:** "I want to actually speak the language at work. Duolingo taught me words but I can't hold a conversation with a client."
**Sales counter:** "Duolingo is great for starting. Berlitz is for finishing -- with human coaching, business content, and certification."

### vs Speak
**When we lose:** Learners who only want AI conversation practice at the lowest price.
**When we win:** "I want AI practice AND human coaching. Speak's AI is great but I need a real teacher sometimes."
**Sales counter:** "We match Speak's AI quality and add what they can't -- certified human instructors, CEFR outcomes, and enterprise reporting."

### vs Praktika
**When we lose:** Casual learners who love the avatar experience and don't need human coaching.
**When we win:** "I want avatar practice that actually leads somewhere -- structured outcomes, certification, business relevance."
**Sales counter:** "Praktika proves avatars work for engagement. Berlitz avatars work for outcomes -- with curriculum, assessment, and human coaching."

### vs Preply
**When we lose:** Learners who want maximum tutor choice and flexibility; enterprises already committed to Preply Business.
**When we win:** "I want consistent quality, not marketplace roulette. And I want AI practice between sessions."
**Sales counter:** "Preply gives you a tutor marketplace. Berlitz gives you a learning system -- AI practice daily, certified teachers weekly, outcomes measured quarterly."

### vs Rosetta Stone
**When we lose:** Government/military buyers locked into GSA schedules; risk-averse enterprises renewing existing contracts.
**When we win:** "Our team tried Rosetta Stone and nobody used it. The technology feels 10 years old."
**Sales counter:** "Everything Rosetta Stone promised, now delivered with modern AI and human coaching. Same compliance depth, 10x better experience."

### vs EF English Live
**When we lose:** Large multinationals with existing EF relationships; buyers who want English-only.
**When we win:** "EF is expensive and the self-study feels dated. We need AI practice, not just courseware."
**Sales counter:** "EF costs $130+/mo for what Berlitz delivers at $99/mo -- plus AI avatar practice, credit flexibility, and programs beyond just English."

### vs Babbel
**When we lose:** European buyers who trust the Babbel brand; budget-conscious learners.
**When we win:** "I finished Babbel's courses but I still can't have a real conversation. I need human practice."
**Sales counter:** "Babbel teaches you about a language. Berlitz teaches you to use it -- with AI conversation practice and human coaching."

### vs Lingoda
**When we lose:** Learners who want intensive live class schedules (Sprint model).
**When we win:** "Lingoda classes are good but I need daily practice between them. And the Sprint rules are confusing."
**Sales counter:** "All the live teaching quality of Lingoda, plus unlimited AI practice between classes, at a lower monthly cost."

### vs ELSA
**When we lose:** Learners focused purely on English pronunciation improvement.
**When we win:** "ELSA improved my pronunciation but I still can't hold a conversation. I need more than pronunciation scores."
**Sales counter:** "ELSA is a pronunciation tool. Berlitz is a communication platform -- pronunciation, conversation, business skills, and human coaching."

### vs Busuu
**When we lose:** Budget learners who value CEFR certificates at low cost.
**When we win:** "Busuu's certificates feel lightweight. I need something my employer will take seriously."
**Sales counter:** "Busuu certificates come from app exercises. Berlitz certificates come from human-assessed, CEFR-aligned learning with 140 years of credibility."

### vs italki
**When we lose:** Self-directed learners who want maximum flexibility and low commitment.
**When we win:** "I booked 50 italki sessions but made no progress because there was no plan. I need structure."
**Sales counter:** "italki is a tutor directory. Berlitz is a learning system -- structured curriculum, AI practice, progress tracking, and certified outcomes."

### vs TalkPal
**When we lose:** Budget learners who want AI conversation in many languages.
**When we win:** "TalkPal is fun to chat with but I'm not actually learning. There's no structure or feedback."
**Sales counter:** "TalkPal is a chat wrapper on GPT. Berlitz is a learning platform -- structured outcomes, human coaching, and business content."
