The Berlitz conversion target of 18–20% is achievable but above Duolingo's rate. The reason it's justified: Berlitz's target user (career-motivated, specific outcome goal, willing to pay for quality) has a fundamentally different conversion profile than Duolingo's mass consumer. Preply and Italki — which also target outcome-motivated learners — convert 25–40% of signups. The 18–20% target is conservative relative to the peer group of outcome-motivated platforms.

The NRR gap is the most important commercial metric to track. Berlitz current NRR is estimated at 90–95% — meaning the base is shrinking, not growing. Every percentage point of NRR improvement above 100% compounds. At 115% NRR, a $10M ARR enterprise base becomes $11.5M in year 2 without a single new logo. The path to 115%+ NRR is Skills and Culture cross-sell on existing Language accounts — that is the expansion motion that drives net retention above 100%.
The gross margin gap vs pure SaaS benchmarks (70–80%) is structural and expected. Berlitz's model includes human teacher sessions (COGS ~$8.32/session) which pure SaaS doesn't have. The right benchmark is hybrid edtech platforms like Preply Business (~35%) and Coursera for Business (~62%). Berlitz at 60–65% target gross margin is strong for a hybrid human+AI model.

Pro gross margin at 41% looks low vs SaaS benchmarks, but it is right for a hybrid model. Preply's blended gross margin is ~30–35% (marketplace take rate). Berlitz Pro at 41% is better. The path to improving it: (1) increase Pro ARPU by adding higher credit tiers ($69 and $99/mo), (2) reduce teacher session COGS over time by recruiting lower-cost IST-compatible teachers for India, (3) grow the AI session mix within Pro (AI Tutor sessions have 10× the margin of teacher sessions). The margin expands as the AI mix grows.

The two metrics where Berlitz targets are most differentiated from benchmarks: LTV:CAC at 12–18:1 (vs 5–8:1 typical) is achievable because CAC is very low in India ($3–6 via employer partnerships and social) and LTV at low churn is long. AI cost as % of paid revenue at 3–6% is better than Duolingo Max (10–20%) because Berlitz uses Claude Haiku for free-tier practice and Sonnet only for structured paid sessions — a tiered model architecture that Duolingo doesn't yet have.
The one metric where Berlitz is currently below benchmark: Enterprise NRR estimated at 90–95% today (base is shrinking). Every other target is achievable from the current position. NRR above 100% requires Skills and Culture cross-sell to be live — which is a 2027 deliverable. The enterprise NRR target of 115% should not be assessed until 12 months after Skills bundles are in the market.
